About

Name

Aprici (pronounced a-PREE-chee) is Latin, from the adjective apricus meaning "warmed by the sun, open to the sun, basking in sunlight." The genitive form aprici means "of the sun-warmed."

Tagline

Warmed by the sun.

Logo — The Confluence Mark

The Aprici mark consists of three overlapping translucent circles converging to a bright center point. It represents warmth gathering and intensifying — where light converges, it grows stronger. The three circles can represent convergence, community, or the three-part meaning of the name (sun, warmth, openness).

Logo Construction

The Confluence mark is built from exactly three overlapping circles of equal radius, arranged in a triangular formation. The center convergence point is marked by a smaller solid circle (the "warmth core") with a bright center dot (the "sun point").

Clear Space

Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the radius of the sun point (5 units in the 200×200 viewbox, or 2.5% of the mark's width) on all sides. No text, imagery, or other elements should enter this zone.

Minimum Size

The mark should not be reproduced smaller than 24px × 24px on screen or 8mm × 8mm in print. Below this size, use the sun point alone as a favicon or micro-mark.

Usage Notes

Preferred Backgrounds

The mark works best on Deep Earth (#0c0a08), Charcoal (#1e1a15), Warm White (#faf4e8), or pure white (#ffffff). Avoid placing the mark on mid-tone backgrounds where the translucent circles lose contrast.

Wordmark Font

The wordmark "Aprici" is always set in Cormorant Light (300 weight) with letter-spacing of 0.05em. Never set the wordmark in bold, all-caps, or a different typeface.

Do Not

Do not rotate the mark. Do not change the circle proportions or positions. Do not apply drop shadows. Do not use the mark on busy photographic backgrounds without a solid color backing. Do not change the gold color to another hue. Do not add outlines to the filled version or fills to the outline version.
